What are STCs?

STCs (Small-scale Technology Certificates) are a federal rebate for small-scale renewable energy systems under the Small-scale Renewable Energy Scheme (SRES), administered by the Clean Energy Regulator. Each STC represents 1 MWh of renewable energy generated over the system's lifetime.

Eligibility

  • Solar PV systems under 100kW
  • Must be installed by NETCC-accredited installer (like Suntech)
  • System must be new and compliant with Australian standards
  • No income or property value limits
  • Available Australia-wide, including Victoria
  • Can be combined with VEU and Solar Homes rebates in Victoria

How STCs Work

STCs provide an upfront discount on your system cost. The rebate value is based on your location, system size, and 'deeming period' (years of expected output). The rebate is deducted by your installer at the time of purchase.

Important: STC values decrease annually as the program phases out by 2030. Installing sooner means higher rebate values.

Location Zones

Australia is divided into STC zones based on solar irradiance. Victoria is primarily in Zone 4, which affects the number of STCs your system generates:

  • Zone 4 (Most of Victoria): Standard STC allocation
  • Zone 3 (Northern Victoria): Slightly higher STC allocation
